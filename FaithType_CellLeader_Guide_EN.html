01   What Is This Tool?

What is FaithType?

The Faith Type Counseling System (FaithType) is a tool that uses 74 questions to help cell leaders understand each member's faith journey and walk alongside them in a way that fits each individual.

Psychological Test ✗
It does not measure the depth or level of one's faith.
Conversation Tool ✓
It is a starting point for deeper conversation with your cell members.
Companion Map ✓
Uses 8 types to find the right direction for each person.

What's Included in This System

①
Faith Type Assessment
74-question assessment completed by the cell member · approx. 15 minutes · available on smartphone and PC
②
Counseling Guide
Cell leader-exclusive counseling reference for all 8 types · organized in 8 sections
③
Growth Plan Sheet
1-year growth roadmap · can be saved as PDF
④
Type-Specific Activity Worksheets
Practice worksheets tailored to each faith type · 2 versions provided per type
Key Principle  —  This system is not a tool for the cell leader to analyze their members. It is a tool for walking together and having conversations together. Conversation always comes before results; listening always comes before interpretation.

02   The Overall Flow at a Glance

Overview
💡 Core Principle  —  You do not need to complete every step on the same day. Allow enough time between each step, and move to the next only after the cell member has had time to understand and process it. Not rushing is the most important thing.
1
Take the Assessment
The cell member completes the Faith Type Assessment on their own.
  • Access the portal via smartphone or PC → select the Navigator (Counselee) tab
  • Takes approx. 15 minutes · respond honestly and quickly, going with your first feeling
  • Save the results as a JPG (image) or PDF for your records
2
Read the Counseling Guide — Cell Leader Preparation
Before meeting with the cell member, the cell leader reads the guide for that type slowly and thoroughly in advance. It's fine to read it one section at a time.
  • Focus on the page for your cell member's type
  • Familiarize yourself with the recommended sample questions (Section 05)
  • Study the patterns to watch for (Section 06) and the approach principles (Section 03)
  • You don't need to read the entire guide at once — reading just the key sections the night before a meeting is sufficient
3
Share Together
Meet with the cell member to share the results and start a conversation.
  • Start with: "How did you feel when you saw the results?"
  • Listen to the cell member's feelings before offering your interpretation
  • Don't try to cover everything in a single meeting
  • The first meeting should be 30–45 minutes; it's fine to spread it over 2–3 sessions
4
Choose and Work Through Worksheets
After sufficient conversation, choose a worksheet together that fits the type. You don't have to start it on the same day.
  • The cell member chooses from 2 worksheet options for their type
  • Worksheets are "conversation material," not "homework"
  • At the next meeting, read through it together and discuss
5
Write the Growth Plan
After experiencing the worksheets, write a 1-year growth roadmap together. This connects naturally at the very end of the process.
  • Write goals together using the cell member's own words
  • Review it together every 3 months and check for change and growth

03   Step-by-Step Instructions

Step-by-Step Guide
① Taking the Assessment
How to Access  —  Go to the portal page (index.html) → select the Navigator (Counselee) tab → click Faith Type Assessment
Before the Assessment
Explain in advance that this is a tool for faith conversation, not a psychological test.
How to Answer
Instruct them to respond honestly and quickly, going with their first instinct.
Saving Results
After completing, save as JPG or PDF. This will be used when sharing with the cell leader.
Time Required
Approx. 15 minutes. It is recommended to complete it alone in a quiet environment where you can focus.
② Reviewing Results — Let the Cell Member Absorb Them
▸ Don't rush them to share on the day they receive the results. Give them a day or two to read it on their own first.
▸ The results screen includes faith type, calling type, aptitudes, and scores by domain.
▸ Leave just one question for the next meeting: "What part resonated with you the most?"
▸ Only after the cell member has fully absorbed the results will the next stage of conversation be rich.

03   Step-by-Step Instructions (continued)

Step-by-Step Guide
④ Sharing Together
◆ Starting the Conversation Begin with: "How did you feel when you saw the results?" Listen to the cell member's feelings before offering your interpretation.
◆ Using Questions Naturally incorporate the recommended questions from Guide Section 05. You don't have to use all of them.
◆ Time Allocation 30–45 minutes is ideal for the first meeting. It's fine to spread the conversation over 2–3 sessions. Don't try to cover everything at once.
◆ Closing Create natural continuity with: "Could we talk about one more thing next time?"
⑤ Choosing and Working Through Worksheets

Choose the worksheet after sufficient conversation has taken place. There's no need to start it on the same day — it's fine to choose it at the next meeting.

●There are 2 worksheets per type. Let the cell member choose the one they want.
●Worksheets are not "homework." Help them choose something they can do without pressure.
●Praise and encourage their participation itself, more than the quality of completion.
●The key is reading through the worksheet together and sharing at the next meeting.
●After finishing one worksheet, you may move on to the next. Don't rush.
⑥ Writing the Growth Plan Sheet

The growth plan sheet connects naturally after the assessment, conversation, and worksheets have all built up. You don't need to complete it at the very first meeting.

When to Complete
After 1–2 worksheet sessions, let it flow naturally into this step.
Complete Together
Fill in the plan based on the content of your conversations with the cell member.
3-Month Check-In
Every 3 months, open the plan together, observe changes, and offer encouragement.

04   Summary of the 8 Faith Types

8 Faith Types

These are the 8 types that appear in the assessment results. Get a basic overview of the relevant type before counseling. Detailed descriptions for each type can be found in the counseling guide.

Roots Type
A person whose faith and life are deeply connected
Their faith is already deep. What they need is not to add more, but to rest and share.
Seed Type
An early-stage believer full of potential
They have a warm character, but their roots in the Word are still shallow. Welcome their questions.
Ground Type
A believer in a good environment
They are benefiting from their surroundings. Help them develop inner conviction.
Fruit Type
A believer bearing fruit in daily life
Their faith is mature. They need to recover freshness and find opportunities to give and share.
Wilderness Type
A believer passing through a difficult environment
Their circumstances are very difficult. Taking small steps and maintaining connection are what matter most.
Seeker Type
A believer searching for truth
They don't yet have certainty. Don't judge their questions — explore them together.
Endurance Type
A person holding on to faith even while exhausted
They are deeply tired. Give them permission to rest first. Don't ask them to do more.
Preparation Type
A person taking their first steps in faith
Everything is new and unfamiliar. A warm welcome and a positive first experience are essential.
All types are equal.   No type is better or worse than another. Each type simply reflects where that person is on their faith journey right now. Do not compare types or rank them in order.

05   Common Mistakes & Cautions

Common Mistakes & Cautions
Mistake 01
Assuming "Because they're this type, they must be like this"
Assessment results are reference material. People are more complex than their results. The results do not fully describe a cell member. Before stating the result, ask first: "How do you feel about this part?"
Mistake 02
Trying to complete all steps at once
If you try to finish the assessment, results review, conversation, worksheets, and growth plan all in one sitting, the cell member will feel overwhelmed. Allow enough time between each step and give the member space to absorb it all.
Mistake 03
Comparing results with other cell members
Avoid ranking or comparing types — for example, "Brother A is a Roots Type and Sister B is a Seed Type, so..." All types are equal and reflect each person's current journey.
Mistake 04
Giving worksheets as "homework"
Saying "Please have this done by next week" creates pressure. Instead, suggest lightly: "Try it once this week, and we can talk about how it went next time." If they haven't done it, "That's okay" comes first.
Mistake 05
The cell leader interpreting and delivering results alone
The counseling guide is for the cell leader to prepare. In the actual conversation, you must listen to the cell member's story first. If you deliver your interpretation first, the space for the member to share their own story disappears.
Caution 01
Encouraging Wilderness Type or Endurance Type members to do more faith activities
These two types are already exhausted or in difficult circumstances. The message that they need to do more only adds burden. "You are enough just as you are right now" comes first. If you see signs of serious emotional difficulty, consider connecting them with a professional counselor.
Caution 02
The cell leader trying to handle everything alone
This system is not a tool for the cell leader to become a professional counselor. What a cell leader can do is "be present" and "listen." If you feel overwhelmed, it is right to ask the designated ministry leader for help.

06   Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Frequently Asked Questions
The result doesn't feel like me. What should I do?
Results can vary depending on your condition or state of mind on the day of the assessment. It is recommended to retake it after some time has passed. The conversation "Which part resonates with you most right now?" is more important than the result itself.
Two types came out very similarly for me.
Both types may reflect who you are right now. Read the descriptions for both types and focus your conversation on the parts that resonate more. The goal is not to find one single precise type.
My cell member is reluctant to share their results.
Don't push. Say first: "You don't have to share if you're not comfortable." Meaningful conversation is still possible without the results. Once trust is built, sharing will happen naturally.
Can the type change over time?
Yes, it can. If their environment or faith journey changes, the type may shift as well. It is recommended to retake the assessment after a year to confirm any changes together.
Is it okay to tell other people about the assessment results?
Do not share the results with others without the cell member's consent. Assessment results are personal information, and conversation must be built on trust.
I'm worried about my Wilderness Type or Endurance Type cell member.
Don't rush to fix things — listen fully first. If you observe signs of serious emotional difficulty, consider connecting them with a professional counselor. The cell leader does not need to handle everything alone.
The cell member hasn't done the worksheet.
Say "That's okay — you can do it next time." Talking about why it was difficult can itself become a better conversation.
